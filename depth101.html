Focal Depth 101

The focal depth refers to the depth at which an earthquake occurs. Earthquakes occurring at a depth of less than 70 km are classified as shallow-focus earthquakes, while those with a focal depth between 70 km and 300 km are commonly termed intermediate-depth earthquakes. Deep-focus earthquakes may occur at much greater depths in the mantle, ranging from 300 km up to 700 km, where older and colder oceanic crust descends beneath another tectonic plate.
